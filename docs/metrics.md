# ATLAS Metrics Framework

ATLAS evaluates AI reliability across four measurable dimensions.

---

# 1. Trust

Trust measures whether an AI system behaves safely, fairly, and consistently.

## Example Metrics

- Safety Compliance Rate
- Hallucination Control Score
- Bias Control Score
- Toxicity Avoidance
- Policy Alignment
- Output Consistency

## Why It Matters

AI systems can be online yet unsafe or misleading.

---

# 2. Latency

Latency measures whether an AI system responds fast enough under production load.

## Example Metrics

- P50 Response Time
- P95 Latency
- P99 Latency
- Throughput per Second
- Queue Delay

## Why It Matters

Slow AI can fail business workflows even if accurate.

---

# 3. Accuracy

Accuracy measures whether outputs are correct and useful.

## Example Metrics

- Precision
- Recall
- F1 Score
- Calibration Score
- Ground Truth Match Rate

## Why It Matters

Incorrect AI outputs create direct business risk.

---

# 4. Stability

Stability measures long-term reliability under changing conditions.

## Example Metrics

- Drift Resistance
- Performance Resilience
- Adversarial Robustness
- Degradation Control
- Distribution Shift Handling

## Why It Matters

Many AI systems fail gradually and silently over time.

---

# Final Goal

ATLAS converts raw AI performance into an operational reliability standard.
