# ATLAS Case Study: Banking AI Risk Scoring

## Context

Banks use AI systems for fraud detection, credit risk, customer support, transaction monitoring, and compliance workflows.

Reliability failures in banking AI can create financial loss, regulatory exposure, and reputational damage.

## Problem

A banking AI system can meet uptime SLAs while still failing through:

- Incorrect fraud scores
- Biased decision outputs
- Latency spikes during high transaction volume
- Drift from evolving fraud behavior
- Unsafe or non-compliant recommendations

## ATLAS Application

| Dimension | Example Measurement |
|---|---|
| Trust | Fairness, safety, compliance alignment |
| Latency | Real-time scoring under peak transaction load |
| Accuracy | Fraud precision, recall, F1 score |
| Stability | Drift and adversarial robustness |

## Business Impact

ATLAS supports:

- Safer AI deployment
- Stronger compliance posture
- Better model monitoring
- Faster risk detection
- Improved executive visibility into AI reliability

## Result

ATLAS enables banking teams to treat AI reliability as a measurable operational discipline rather than an informal model-quality assumption.
